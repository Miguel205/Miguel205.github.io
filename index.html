Copper etching

In chemistry we were assigned to make an illustration to etch on a piece of Copper.

Illustration

The first thing we did, was creating an image. We could either draw it on a piece of paper or create it online; I chose to use adobe illustrator. I made a hand touching electricity which I wanted to be the symbol of the countless hours that Tesla invested experimenting while developing alternating current (AC). The next step was printing the file on a special film and then, it was ironed on to a copper sheet.

Then, both pieces of metal, the copper plate and a piece of aluminum were submerged into a bin with salt water and were conected to a power supply

The power supply provokes the ionization of the copper atoms, which means that the copper atoms become positively charged copper ions Cu++ and leave the copper plate. This process corrodes everything other than the protected copper and finally we have our copper etching image.
